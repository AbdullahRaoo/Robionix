# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Factory owners and operations/production directors of Pakistani manufacturing — textile,
knitwear, sports goods, surgical, leather. They are evaluating whether to trust a local
deep-tech vendor over expensive imported ERP (SAP/Oracle/Dynamics). They are skeptical of
"tech startup" fluff and moved by proof, uptime, ROI, export competitiveness, and local
support. Secondary: apparel-buyer QA teams, event organizers (Bib Recognition), and
academic/industry partners (NUTECH).

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Robionix Technologies — a Pakistani industrial automation house that builds
hardware + software for manufacturers: MagicQC (AI garment-size measurement station),
manufacturing ERP (RMES/KMES), computer vision, robotics, embedded systems. The site must
establish serious-engineering credibility within seconds, showcase MagicQC as live proof, and
convert factory decision-makers into booked meetings.

## Brand Personality

Engineered, proven, plain-spoken, nationally ambitious. Three words: precise, credible,
purposeful. Voice leads with outcomes and numbers, shows the real machine (dashboards, factory
floors, operators), and carries a restrained current of national purpose (cutting the import
bill, growing exports) — conviction, not slogans. Never hypey, never agency-speak.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS-startup landing pages (Inter on white, hero-metric template, identical icon-card grids).
- "Full-service digital agency" framing — undersells the engineering.
- Decorative gradients, glows, aurora blobs, gradient text (the AI tells the owner explicitly rejected before).
- Editorial-magazine cosplay (display-serif + italic + drop caps) on a brief that isn't a magazine.
- Overclaiming brand relationships; buyer names appear only as honest tolerance-profile references.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the machine.** Real product UI, dashboards, and factory photography carry the page — not abstraction.
2. **Proof over adjectives.** Numbers, named industries, and live system shots instead of "innovative/cutting-edge."
3. **Amber is earned.** Petrol carries structure; the single amber accent marks only what matters (one CTA, one data point).
4. **Engineering legibility.** Strong hierarchy, generous space, plain language — a factory director gets it in 3 seconds.
5. **National ambition, stated quietly.** The exports/trade-deficit mission is conviction in the copy, never a slogan banner.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1 against the warm-paper base. Never
convey state by color alone (pass/fail needs text/icon too). Full `prefers-reduced-motion`
alternative for every animation. Keyboard-navigable, visible focus states. Primary audience
may read English as a second language — plain, concrete copy; consider EN+Urdu later.
